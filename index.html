Core Rules
The Battle Round Core Rule
Both players take a turn. 5 phases per turn. Game ends after Round 5 or army is destroyed.
Battle-Shock ⚠ Core Rule
#1 most forgotten rule. Units below half strength roll 2D6 vs Leadership each Command Phase.
Objective Control Core Rule
Compare OC totals within 3". Battle-shocked units have OC 0.
Modifiers & Limits Core Rule
No roll can be modified by more than +1 or −1. Natural 1 always fails, natural 6 always hits/wounds.
Transports Core Rule
Embark within 3". Disembark in Movement Phase. Destroyed transport — bail out or risk mortal wounds.
Strategic Reserves Core Rule
Cannot arrive Turn 1. Set up within 6" of any edge, 9"+ from enemies. Left in reserve = destroyed.
Core Concepts
Line of Sight Concept
True line of sight — model's eye view. Any part visible to any part = visible.
Engagement Range Concept
Within 1" horizontally and 5" vertically. In melee = cannot shoot, cannot Normal move.
Unit Coherency Concept
All models within 2" of one other (7+ models: within 2" of two others).
Attack Sequence Concept
Hit → Wound → Allocate → Save → Damage. Unmodified 1 always fails, 6 always hits/wounds.
Turn Phases
Command Phase Phase 1
Gain 1CP each. Take Battle-shock tests for units below half strength.
Movement Phase Phase 2
Normal move, Advance (no shoot/charge), Fall Back, or Remain Stationary.
Shooting Phase Phase 3
Declare all targets before rolling. Cannot shoot if Advanced, Fell Back, or in Engagement Range.
Charge Phase Phase 4
Declare charge, roll 2D6. Must reach all declared targets. Enemy may Overwatch.
Fight Phase Phase 5
Fights First units go first. Pile in 3", fight, consolidate 3". Alternate choosing units.
Terrain
Benefit of Cover Terrain
+1 to armour saves vs ranged attacks. Does not apply to 3+ saves vs AP 0.
Ruins Terrain
Infantry wholly within = Cover. Ground floor walls block line of sight.
Craters & Rubble Terrain
Infantry wholly on = Cover. Does not block line of sight.
Barricades Terrain
Infantry within 3" and partially hidden = Cover. Can fight through/over.
Hills & Structures Terrain
Models can stand on top. No automatic cover. Better line of sight from height.
